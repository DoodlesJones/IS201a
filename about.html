About Me

My name, my family, my dream

My name is Trixie Petunia Pineapple Judd. I am the third oldest from a family of 17. Because of that fact alone, I have had constant opportunities to develop my skills of problem solving and efficiency.

Problem solving: In 2015, a childhood dream came true when my family finally bought milk cows. There was just one problem: no one in our family knew how to milk a cow! The challenge was on. Three hours later with barely half a cup of milk collected, the scorecard read Trixie: 0, cow: 1. Sweaty and bewildered, I took a step back to analyze the situation. I started with the question, “Why? What is preventing me from accomplishing my goal?”, then, “How? How can I remove this obstacle?” I determined the biggest obstacle was the cow’s movement, so the next day I got to work constructing the solution: a stanchion.

Efficiency: A story my dad loves to tell is about a time when I was just six years old. My mom had consistently struggled to clean her room in under 30 minutes when one day she seemingly made the realization that if she pretended to be a robot, she could cut the time down to 20 minutes. According to my dad, I listened patiently to my mom and then explained, “Oh, that’s nice. But you can clean your room in under five minutes if you simply gather everything into one big pile, separate it into smaller piles depending on where they go, and then go put those piles away. That way you know you haven’t missed anything, and you don’t have to take multiple trips.” That six-year-old wisdom has only grown stronger, and my mom has been following my solution ever since.
